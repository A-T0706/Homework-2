About

A short story about how piano became the place I focus, grow, and express myself.

How it started

I began piano by learning the basics: posture, finger numbers, and reading simple notes. At first it felt like multitasking — eyes on the music, hands on the keys, and brain trying to keep up.

Over time, patterns started to appear. Chords made sense, rhythms became familiar, and I realized piano is like learning a new language — one that can say what words can’t.

How I practice

My favorite way to improve is to break a piece into tiny parts: a few measures at a time, slow tempo, hands separate, then together.

I focus on clean sound, consistent rhythm, and expressive dynamics — the “story” of the music. When something is difficult, repetition matters, but smart repetition matters more.

What I’m working on

  • Accuracy at faster tempos
  • Expressive dynamics (soft vs powerful)
  • Left-hand control and balance
  • Confidence performing
